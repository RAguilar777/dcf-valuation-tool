## Results

Based on financial data pulled directly from the Financial Modeling Prep API, Apple Inc. (AAPL) was modelled using a 5-year free cash flow projection at a 6% annual growth rate, discounted at a WACC of approximately 9%. A terminal growth rate of 2.5% was applied, remaining consistent with long-run nominal GDP growth expectations.

The model outputs an intrinsic value of **$162.58 per share** under base case assumptions. As of the time of this analysis, Apple trades at a meaningful premium to this estimate, suggesting the market is pricing in either stronger long-term growth, margin expansion, or a structural scarcity premium given Apple's brand and ecosystem dominance.

The sensitivity analysis highlights that intrinsic value is highly sensitive to WACC and terminal growth assumptions — a 1% shift in either variable produces a wide range of outcomes. This shows why DCF models are best interpreted as a range of values rather than a single definitive number.
